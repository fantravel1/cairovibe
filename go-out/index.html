Nightlife Guide

Going Out in Cairo

Cairo's nightlife starts late. Most venues begin filling up around 11 PM-midnight, with peak hours between 1-3 AM. Clubs often stay open until 4-5 AM, sometimes later on weekends. Don't show up to a club before midnight - it'll be empty. Pre-drinks at a bar or rooftop from 9-11 PM is the typical start to a night out.

Thursday and Friday nights are the biggest nights out in Cairo (Egypt's weekend is Friday-Saturday). Wednesday is also popular for after-work drinks. Tuesday night often features special events at Cairo Jazz Club. Sunday-Monday are quieter but can be great for finding a chill vibe without the crowds.

Costs vary widely. Cover charges range from 200-500 EGP ($6-16 USD) for clubs, often including a drink. Drinks at upscale venues run 150-300 EGP ($5-10) for cocktails, less for beer. A full night out including dinner, drinks, and club entry might cost 1000-2000 EGP ($30-65) at mid-range venues, more at luxury spots.

Cairo nightlife is generally safe, especially in areas like Zamalek, Maadi, and New Cairo. Stick to established venues, use Uber or Careem for transport, and stay aware of your surroundings. Women may experience some unwanted attention but venues in tourist areas are accustomed to international guests. Going out in groups is always recommended.
