Cairo Street Food Guide

Everything you need to eat on the streets of Cairo - the dishes, the spots, and the prices. All under EGP 50.

📖 8 min read 💰 Budget Friendly 🌱 Vegetarian Options

Cairo's street food scene is one of the world's great culinary experiences - and one of its cheapest. For less than a few dollars, you can eat like royalty. Here's everything you need to know.

🍜 Koshary

Egypt's national dish. Rice, pasta, lentils, chickpeas, crispy onions, tomato sauce, and garlic vinegar. It's vegan, filling, and absolutely delicious.

Price:

EGP 25-60

Best at:

Koshary Abou Tarek, Downtown

Pro tip: Always add the garlic vinegar - it makes the dish.

🥜 Ful & Taameya

Ful medames is slow-cooked fava beans mashed with garlic, lemon, and olive oil. Taameya is Egyptian falafel made with fava beans (not chickpeas like elsewhere). Together in fresh baladi bread, they're breakfast perfection.

Price:

EGP 10-25 for a sandwich

Best at:

Gad, Zooba, any neighborhood fuul cart

Pro tip: Ask for "ful iskandarani" for the Alexandria-style with tahini and cumin.

🥙 Shawarma

Thin-sliced meat (usually chicken or beef) from a rotating spit, wrapped in bread with tahini, pickles, and garlic sauce. Cairo's shawarma is legendary.

Price:

EGP 30-50

Best at:

Kazaz, Arabiata, Shawarma El Reem

Pro tip: "Syrian style" means more garlic sauce. Always say yes.

🍖 Hawawshi

Spiced minced meat baked inside baladi bread until the bread is crispy and the meat is juicy. It's essentially an Egyptian meat pie and it's incredible.

Price:

EGP 25-45

Best at:

Sobhy Kaber, neighborhood bakeries

Pro tip: Eat it fresh and hot - hawawshi doesn't travel well.

🥧 Fiteer

Egyptian layered pastry that can be sweet or savory. Watching a fiteer maker stretch the dough paper-thin is a show in itself. Sweet versions come with honey, cream, or Nutella; savory with cheese, meat, or eggs.

Price:

EGP 40-80

Best at:

El Abd, Fatatri El Tahrir

Pro tip: The "mixed" (meshalltet) with cream and honey is dangerously good.

🧃 Street Drinks

Sugarcane Juice

Fresh-pressed on the street. Sweet, refreshing, and natural.

EGP 10-15

Karkade

Hibiscus tea, served hot or cold. Deep red and tangy.

EGP 10-20

Sahlab

Warm, creamy orchid root drink. Winter comfort.

EGP 15-25

💡 Street Food Safety Tips

  • Eat where locals eat - busy stalls mean fresh food
  • Watch the food being prepared when possible
  • Avoid pre-cut fruit and salads from street vendors
  • Stick to bottled water
  • Cooked food served hot is generally safe
  • Start slowly if your stomach isn't used to street food
