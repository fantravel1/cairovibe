Cairo Cafe Guide

An ahwa (Arabic for coffee) is a traditional Egyptian coffeehouse. These local gathering spots serve Turkish coffee, tea, and shisha. You'll find men playing backgammon and dominoes, watching football, and socializing. Ahwas are central to Cairo's social fabric - order a Turkish coffee or mint tea, and soak in the atmosphere.

For reliable WiFi and work-friendly environments, head to specialty coffee shops like Left Bank or Qahwa, or chains like Cilantro and Harris Cafe. The Greek Campus Downtown has great co-working spaces. Zamalek and Maadi have the best concentration of laptop-friendly cafes. Always have a backup mobile data plan.

Yes! Cairo's cafe culture is 24/7. Traditional ahwas often stay open all night, and chains like Cilantro have 24-hour locations. Even specialty coffee shops stay open until 11 PM-midnight. Late-night cafe sessions are a big part of Cairo life, especially on weekends.

Shisha (hookah) is widely available at traditional ahwas and many modern cafes. Traditional spots offer the best experience and prices. Popular flavors include apple, grape, and mint. Most cafes allow you to stay for hours with just a shisha order. Some upscale venues and specialty coffee shops don't offer shisha.
