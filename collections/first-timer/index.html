First Time in Cairo

📖 Complete Guide 👥 New Visitors

Welcome to Cairo - one of the world's most intense, beautiful, chaotic, and unforgettable cities. This guide will help you navigate your first visit with confidence, from arrival to departure.

What to Expect

Cairo is sensory overload in the best way. 20+ million people, 5,000 years of history, incredible food, and a nightlife scene that rivals any global city. It's loud, it's busy, it's occasionally frustrating - and it will capture your heart.

The key to enjoying Cairo is embracing the chaos rather than fighting it. Traffic is part of the experience. Haggling is a sport. Dinner starts at 10 PM. Once you adjust your expectations, the magic reveals itself.

Must-Do Experiences

1. The Pyramids & Sphinx

Go early morning (7 AM opening) or late afternoon. The only surviving Ancient Wonder. Nothing prepares you for seeing them in person.

2. Egyptian Museum

Tutankhamun's treasures, mummies, and millennia of artifacts. Allow 3+ hours. The new Grand Egyptian Museum will be even more impressive.

3. Khan El Khalili

Medieval bazaar experience. Go in the evening when it's magical. Haggle for everything. Stop for tea at El Fishawi.

4. Sunset on the Nile

Felucca ride or rooftop cocktails - watching the sun set over the Nile is mandatory. Sequoia or Crimson are perfect for drinks.

5. Try Egyptian Street Food

Koshari, ful, taameya, shawarma - eat like a local. Zooba for elevated versions, street vendors for authentic.

Where to Stay

  • Zamalek: Best for nightlife, restaurants, and walking. Island neighborhood, safe and beautiful.
  • Garden City: Luxury hotels, central location, Nile views. More upscale, less street life.
  • Maadi: Quiet, leafy, expat-friendly. Good for families, further from main attractions.
  • Giza: Stay here if Pyramids are your priority. Mena House is unforgettable.

Getting Around

  • Uber/Careem: Your best friend. Cheap, air-conditioned, no haggling. Works everywhere.
  • Metro: Cheap and fast but limited coverage. Useful for Downtown, Heliopolis, Maadi.
  • Taxis: Negotiate price before getting in. Use Uber prices as a benchmark.
  • Walking: Great in Zamalek, Downtown, and Islamic Cairo. Otherwise, stick to wheels.

Money Tips

  • Currency is Egyptian Pound (EGP). ~$1 = EGP 30 (check current rates)
  • ATMs are everywhere - most accept international cards
  • Credit cards accepted at hotels, restaurants, and shops. Street food is cash only.
  • Tipping is expected - 10-15% at restaurants, small tips for services
  • Everything is negotiable in markets and with taxis

Safety & Common Sense

  • Cairo is generally safe for tourists, including at night in main areas
  • Petty theft is rare but keep valuables secure in crowds
  • Ignore touts and "guides" who approach at tourist sites
  • Drink bottled water only
  • Women should dress modestly (knees and shoulders covered) outside hotels
  • Download offline Google Maps before you arrive
