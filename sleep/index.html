Staying in Cairo

For first-time visitors, Zamalek or Garden City are ideal - safe, central, with great restaurants and nightlife. Downtown is best for budget travelers and those wanting authentic Cairo vibes. Maadi suits those seeking quiet, leafy streets and a more residential feel. New Cairo is modern but far from the city center.

Cairo offers excellent value. Hostels start around $10-15/night, budget hotels $30-50, mid-range hotels $60-120, and luxury 5-star hotels $150-400. Prices are significantly lower than comparable cities, and you get more for your money. Book directly for the best rates.

Stay in central Cairo (Zamalek, Downtown, Garden City) unless the Pyramids are your only focus. Central Cairo has better nightlife, restaurants, and is closer to most attractions. You can easily visit the Pyramids on a day trip - they're only 30-45 minutes away. The Giza area near the Pyramids is less developed for tourists.

Airbnb can be great for longer stays or if you want a local neighborhood experience. Zamalek and Maadi have excellent apartments. However, for short stays, hotels often provide better value when you factor in included breakfast, location, and services. Always check recent reviews and communicate clearly with hosts.
