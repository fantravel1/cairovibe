Cairo Food Guide

Eating Like a Local

The most famous Egyptian foods to try in Cairo include Koshari (a hearty mix of rice, pasta, lentils, chickpeas, and spicy tomato sauce), Ful Medames (slow-cooked fava beans), Ta'meya (Egyptian falafel made with fava beans), Molokhia (jute leaf soup), and Shawarma. For the most authentic experience, try these at local street food spots in Downtown Cairo.

Downtown Cairo is the undisputed king of street food. Head to Talaat Harb Square for iconic spots, or explore the alleys around Al-Hussein and Khan el-Khalili for traditional fare. Sayeda Zeinab neighborhood offers incredible local eats. For elevated street food in a modern setting, try Zooba in Zamalek.

Egyptians eat dinner late by Western standards. Most locals don't sit down for dinner until 9-10 PM, and restaurants stay busy until midnight or later. Lunch is typically the biggest meal, eaten between 2-4 PM. If you're dining at a popular restaurant, making reservations for 9 PM or later is common.

Cairo food is generally safe, but use common sense. Stick to busy restaurants with high turnover, drink bottled water, and start slowly with street food to let your stomach adjust. Avoid pre-cut fruit and salads from street vendors. Hot, freshly cooked food is your safest bet. Most travelers have no issues, but bring basic medication just in case.
