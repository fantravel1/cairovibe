Bab El Sharq

★ 4.4 $$ 📍 Heliopolis 🍖 Egyptian & Levantine
Traditional Grills Mezza Live Music

Where Cairo celebrates its culinary heritage. Bab El Sharq (Gate of the East) delivers the full Egyptian and Levantine experience - charcoal-grilled meats, tables laden with mezza, live oud music, and a setting that feels like stepping into a more gracious era.

View Menu Reserve

The Setting

Housed in a beautifully restored Heliopolis villa, Bab El Sharq recreates the grandeur of old Cairo hospitality. High ceilings with intricate Islamic geometric patterns, brass lanterns casting warm light, and a courtyard garden that's magical on cool evenings. The interior design draws on Mamluk and Ottoman influences without feeling kitschy.

On weekends, live musicians play traditional Arabic music - oud, qanun, and the occasional singer performing Um Kalthoum classics. It's the kind of place where dinner becomes an event, where you linger for hours over dishes and conversation.

The Ritual

A proper meal at Bab El Sharq follows the traditional progression: start with an array of cold mezza to nibble with fresh bread, move to hot mezza, then the grilled mains arrive sizzling. End with Arabic coffee and something sweet. Don't rush - this is not fast food culture.

Pro Tips

  • The mixed mezza platter for two is the way to start
  • Thursday and Friday have live music - book the courtyard
  • Portions are generous - order family style
  • The molokhia is made fresh and worth ordering
  • End with Turkish coffee and Um Ali
