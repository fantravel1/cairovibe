The Experience

Sequoia is Cairo's restaurant. Since opening in 2003, it has defined what a night out in the city means. The concept is simple but perfectly executed: a sprawling wooden terrace extending over the Nile, beautiful people, excellent Mediterranean food, and an atmosphere that turns strangers into friends.

During the day, it's a relaxed spot for long lunches. As the sun sets, the magic begins. The lights come on, the crowd builds, and Sequoia transforms into the city's living room. By midnight on a Thursday, every table is full, shisha smoke drifts across the terrace, and the Nile glitters below.

The Terrace

Request a Nile-side table when you book. The terrace extends over the water, with unobstructed views of the river and Giza beyond. Sunset tables (6-8 PM in summer) are the most coveted - book at least a week ahead for weekends. The back terrace is more intimate; the front is where the action is.

Pro Tips

  • Thursday and Friday nights are the busiest - reserve days in advance
  • Ask for the "front terrace, Nile side" for best views
  • The mezze is legendary - order generously for the table
  • Shisha is excellent here, especially the grape mint
  • Dress smart casual - this crowd makes an effort
  • Lunch is more relaxed and easier to book
