Koshary Abou Tarek

★ 4.4 $ 📍 Downtown 🍽 Egypt's #1 Koshary
Koshary Street Food Budget Vegetarian

Four floors. One dish. Zero pretense. Koshary Abou Tarek is where Cairo comes to eat Egypt's national dish - a glorious carb-bomb of rice, pasta, lentils, chickpeas, and crispy onions doused in tomato sauce and garlic vinegar. This is essential Cairo.

How to Order Get Directions

The Experience

Walking into Abou Tarek is like walking into a koshary cathedral. The ground floor is chaos - crowds pushing, staff shouting orders, steam rising from massive pots. Upstairs it's calmer, with seating across four floors. Every table, every customer, eating the same thing: koshary.

This isn't fusion cuisine or farm-to-table philosophy. It's a dish that's fed Cairo's working class for over a century, perfected here since 1950. It's vegan. It's filling. It costs almost nothing. And it's absolutely delicious.

What is Koshary?

Koshary is Egypt's unofficial national dish - a layered bowl of:

  • Rice - the base
  • Macaroni - small elbow pasta
  • Spaghetti - broken into pieces
  • Lentils - brown lentils for earthiness
  • Chickpeas - for protein
  • Tomato sauce - tangy and spiced
  • Crispy fried onions - the crowning glory
  • Garlic vinegar & hot sauce - on the side

The Ritual

  1. Join the queue (it moves fast)
  2. Tell them your size in Arabic or just show fingers (1-4)
  3. Pay at the counter, get a ticket
  4. Find a seat upstairs
  5. Your koshary arrives within minutes
  6. Add garlic vinegar and hot sauce to taste
  7. Eat. Feel alive. Question all your previous life choices.

Pro Tips

  • Go at lunchtime for the full chaotic experience
  • Medium is plenty for most people - it's denser than it looks
  • The garlic vinegar is essential - don't skip it
  • Hot sauce varies from mild to face-melting - start small
  • It's 100% vegan and incredibly cheap
  • Cash only - bring small bills
  • Combine with a Downtown walking tour
