Maadi

🌳 Leafy Streets 💰 $$ Mid-Range 🌍 Expat Hub 🛡 Very Safe

Cairo's greenest neighborhood. Tree-lined streets, a laid-back vibe, and a diverse international community make Maadi the perfect escape from the city's chaos. Think cozy cafes, international cuisine, and casual bars where everyone knows your name.

Explore Venues Where to Stay

The Vibe

Maadi feels like a different city entirely. Originally developed as a garden suburb in the early 1900s, it's kept its leafy character with wide streets, old trees, and a village-like atmosphere. The international schools and embassies have made it a hub for expats, giving it a cosmopolitan feel.

The pace is slower here. People walk their dogs, jog along Road 9, and linger over brunch on weekends. It's family-friendly during the day and quietly social at night - think casual bars rather than clubs.

What to Expect

Maadi is organized around numbered roads and streets. Road 9 is the main commercial artery with most restaurants and cafes. Degla is the upscale southern section. The area near the Metro station (Maadi Metro stop) is more local and budget-friendly.

Getting Around

Maadi has its own Metro stop on Line 1, connecting you to Downtown in about 20 minutes. The neighborhood is walkable, especially around Road 9 and Degla. Uber and Careem work well. Many residents use bikes or walk - it's that kind of place.

Pro Tips

  • Road 9 is the main strip - start there
  • Friday brunch is a big scene here
  • Wadi Degla Protectorate is nearby for hiking
  • The Corniche has great Nile views
  • Maadi Grand Mall for shopping and cinema
