Cairo Essentials

Everything you need to know before your Cairo adventure.

The best nightlife in Cairo is found in Zamalek, Downtown Cairo, and Maadi. Zamalek offers upscale rooftop bars and lounges with stunning Nile views. Downtown has eclectic underground venues, live music spots, and authentic local bars. Maadi features laid-back bars popular with expats and young professionals. New Cairo is home to many of the city's biggest clubs and modern entertainment venues.

The best areas to eat in Cairo include Downtown for authentic street food and local eateries, Zamalek for fine dining and international cuisine, New Cairo for modern food courts and trendy restaurants, and Maadi for diverse international options and cozy cafes. For the best koshari and foul, head to Downtown. For upscale dining, Zamalek and Garden City are your best bets.

Cairo is generally safe for tourists at night, especially in areas like Zamalek, Maadi, and New Cairo. It's recommended to stick to well-lit areas, use reputable transportation services like Uber or Careem, and be aware of your surroundings. Tourist areas are regularly patrolled and locals are typically friendly and helpful. Avoid isolated areas and always trust your instincts.

Dress codes in Cairo vary by venue. Upscale clubs and rooftop bars often require smart casual to formal attire - no shorts, sandals, or sportswear. Men typically wear collared shirts and closed shoes. Women have more flexibility but tend to dress elegantly. Local bars and casual venues are more relaxed. When in doubt, smart casual is always a safe choice.

Cairo's nightlife starts late compared to Western cities. Dinner typically begins around 9-10 PM, bars get busy around 11 PM, and clubs don't really get going until midnight or later. Many venues stay open until 3-4 AM, with some continuing until sunrise. On weekends (Thursday and Friday nights), expect things to run even later. Cairo truly comes alive after dark.

The safest and most convenient way to get around Cairo at night is using ride-hailing apps like Uber and Careem. They're reliable, affordable, and widely available. Traditional taxis (white cabs) are an option but negotiate the fare beforehand. The Cairo Metro runs until midnight. Avoid walking long distances late at night and always have your destination address in Arabic for drivers.
