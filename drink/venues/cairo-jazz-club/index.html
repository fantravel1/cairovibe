Cairo Jazz Club

★ 4.5 $$ 📍 Agouza 🎵 Live Music Nightly
Live Music Indie Jazz Late Night

The heartbeat of Cairo's underground. Since 2001, CJC has been the city's most important music venue - where emerging artists become legends, where Cairo's creative class gathers, and where the best nights of your life happen.

This Week's Shows View Menu

The Legend

Cairo Jazz Club isn't just a venue - it's an institution. For over two decades, this intimate space has nurtured Egypt's music scene, from jazz and blues to indie rock, electronic, and experimental sounds. Walk through the door and you're joining a legacy of nights that have shaped Cairo's cultural identity.

The room is deliberately small - capacity around 200 - which creates an electric intimacy between performers and audience. When a band hits its stride and the crowd moves as one, there's nothing quite like it. This is where Cairene artists are discovered and where international acts come to experience the city's legendary energy.

Drinks

The bar serves a solid selection of beers (Stella, Heineken, craft options), cocktails, and spirits. Prices are reasonable by Cairo standards - expect to pay EGP 80-120 for beers, EGP 150-200 for cocktails. The crowd comes for the music, but the drinks keep the energy flowing.

The Crowd

CJC attracts Cairo's most interesting people - artists, musicians, filmmakers, writers, and anyone who values creativity over pretense. The dress code is whatever you want; the vibe is come-as-you-are. You'll meet strangers, make friends, and leave with stories. This is Cairo at its most authentic.

Pro Tips

  • Arrive by 10 PM to get a good spot for popular acts
  • Friday and Saturday have cover charges (usually EGP 200-400)
  • Follow their Instagram for weekly lineup announcements
  • The back corner has the best sound; front center for intimacy
  • Uber home - parking is a nightmare and drinks will be had
  • Cash is king, though cards are accepted
