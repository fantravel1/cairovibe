Cairo Bar Essentials

Yes, alcohol is legal in Egypt. However, it's primarily served in hotels, licensed bars, restaurants, and nightclubs. You won't find it in local cafes or street food spots. There are also liquor stores called "Drinkies" throughout the city. During Ramadan, many venues restrict alcohol service or close their bars entirely.

The best rooftop bars in Cairo include The Roof at Kempinski Nile Hotel (stunning Nile views), Crimson Bar & Grill in Zamalek, and Windows on the World at Ramses Hilton (panoramic city views). For a more casual vibe, try the rooftops along the Nile in Zamalek. Book ahead for sunset spots on weekends.

Most bars in Cairo open around 5-6 PM and stay busy until 2-3 AM. Hotel bars often stay open later. Things don't really get going until 10-11 PM. On Thursday and Friday nights (the weekend), bars are busiest and stay open later. Sunday-Wednesday tends to be quieter.

Upscale bars and hotel venues often have dress codes - no shorts, sandals, or sportswear. Smart casual is the safest bet: collared shirts for men, elegant casual for women. Casual neighborhood bars and pubs are more relaxed. When in doubt, dress up rather than down.
